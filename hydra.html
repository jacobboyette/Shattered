The lake’s surface shatters with a deafening roar as something massive breaks through, sending waves crashing against the shore. Water sprays like a storm, drenching the air with the sharp, briny scent of the deep. Before you, rising from the depths, are not one but many heads, each serpent-like and crowned with jagged horns. Their eyes glint with a predatory hunger, gleaming like gemstones in the dim light. The creature lets out a bone-rattling hiss, each head moving in perfect, deadly harmony as they scan their surroundings. The stories of the hydra flood your mind—of its unyielding strength and its endless thirst for trespassers. The lake falls eerily silent for a moment, the calm before the strike. The hydra’s many eyes settle on you, and you realize with cold dread that it has chosen its prey.
